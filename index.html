Common questions

Kelowna fishing charter FAQ

How much does a Kelowna fishing charter cost?

Our Kelowna fishing charters are a flat $200 CAD per hour, up to six guests. Rods, reels, tackle and bait are included. Lunch and beverages are available on request for an additional per-person charge.

What fish will we catch?

On Okanagan Lake we primarily target kokanee salmon, rainbow trout and lake trout. Shuswap trips add chinook salmon and burbot. Arrow Lake trips chase bull trout and the famous Gerrard rainbow. We'll tune tactics to what's biting that week.

Do I need a fishing licence?

Yes — anglers 16 and up need a British Columbia freshwater fishing licence. You can buy a one-day, eight-day or annual licence online in a few minutes. We'll send you the link when you book.

Is the charter kid-friendly?

Absolutely. The Kingfisher's heated cabin, onboard head and bench seating make it one of the most comfortable charter boats on Okanagan Lake for families. Life jackets are provided in all sizes.

When is the best time of year?

Okanagan Lake fishes well year-round. Kokanee peaks late spring through summer; lake trout are strong in cool shoulder seasons. Sightseeing is best May through October.
