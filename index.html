Common questions

Kelowna fishing charter FAQ

How much does a Kelowna fishing charter cost?

Our Kelowna fishing charters are a flat $200 CAD per hour, up to six guests. Half-day (4 hr) $800 · Full-day (8 hr) $1,600 · Sunset cruise $400. Rods, reels, tackle and bait are included. Lunch is available for an additional per-person charge.

What fish will we catch?

On Okanagan Lake: kokanee salmon, rainbow trout and lake trout. Shuswap adds chinook salmon and burbot. Arrow Lake adds bull trout and Gerrard rainbow. We tune tactics to what's biting that week.

Do I need a fishing licence?

Yes — anglers 16 and up need a British Columbia freshwater fishing licence. Kids under 16 fish free. See our 3-minute licence guide.

Is the charter kid-friendly?

Absolutely. The Kingfisher's heated cabin, onboard head and bench seating make it one of the most comfortable charter boats on Okanagan Lake for families. Life jackets are provided in all sizes.

When is the best time of year?

Okanagan Lake fishes well year-round. Kokanee peaks late spring through summer; lake trout are strong in cool shoulder seasons. Read the month-by-month guide.
