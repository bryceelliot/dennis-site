South Okanagan water

Penticton's quieter sister to Okanagan Lake

Skaha Lake stretches south from Penticton toward Okanagan Falls — a smaller, calmer lake than its bigger neighbour to the north. Less boat traffic, sandy beaches, vineyard-and-orchard hillsides on both shores, and dramatic granite cliffs on the east side. It's our pick when guests want a private boat day in the south Okanagan without the busy summer wakes of Okanagan Lake.

What you'll see

  • Skaha Bluffs — granite cliffs on the east shore, world-class for climbing photos from the water
  • Okanagan Falls / Vaseux Lake outlet — south end of the lake, calm reflective water
  • Sandy bay swimming stops — Skaha Beach and the south-shore coves
  • Vineyard shoreline — Painted Rock, Blasted Church and other south-Okanagan wineries visible from the boat

Booking a Skaha trip

Skaha outings are custom and run on request — most common for guests staying in Penticton, Naramata or Summerland. Same $200/hr rate, same boat. Best for sightseeing cruises, anniversary outings, group day trips and photographers chasing south-Okanagan light.
