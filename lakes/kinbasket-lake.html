Backcountry water

The most remote water we run

Kinbasket Lake is a 425 km² reservoir formed by the Mica Dam on the Columbia River — a long, fjord-shaped expanse of glacier-fed water hemmed in by the Selkirk Mountains to the west and the Canadian Rockies to the east. There is almost no shoreline development. Most days you'll see more wildlife than people. Cell service is limited; planning matters.

Who Kinbasket is for

Adventure-minded groups who want something genuinely off the map. Photography teams chasing big mountain light. Anglers who want quiet water without crowds. Anyone willing to trade convenience for scenery on a scale you can't get on Okanagan Lake.

What to expect

  • Multi-day only — typical 2–3 day trips, custom itineraries
  • Drive distance — about 4 hours from Kelowna; usually overnight in Revelstoke or Mica Creek the night before
  • Weather window — late June through September, conditions permitting
  • By request — Kinbasket trips book 4+ weeks ahead and are quoted individually based on duration and logistics

Booking a Kinbasket trip

Get in touch with your dates, group size and what you're hoping to do — fishing, photography, just exploring. We'll come back with a custom multi-day quote. Same Kingfisher 2525, same Captain Dennis, just trailered north for the run.
